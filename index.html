Section 01

The Science of Strike Selection

Instead of buying expensive At-The-Money (ATM) options, this strategy deliberately targets cheaper options priced around ₹60 to ₹70. Here's the mathematical reasoning.

The Gamma Advantage

Target Out-of-the-Money options with a Delta of 0.30 to 0.35. At this specific Delta, the option's Gamma is typically at its highest — meaning the Delta itself changes the fastest as the underlying moves.

Asymmetric Risk / Reward

High Gamma means favorable moves make the premium accelerate and multiply rapidly. But if the market moves against you, the initially low Delta ensures the premium doesn't crash as violently.

The Expiry Day Exception

This rule breaks down on expiry day — Theta eats premium too fast. To counter this, abandon the current week's contracts and trade the next week's expiry to retain high Gamma without extreme Theta risk.

Section 02

The 11:00 AM Execution Rule

Patience is a core component of this strategy. Trading the morning rush is dangerous — the data isn't reliable yet. Here's how the morning unfolds.

9:15 AM Observe

Market Opens — Observation Only

The market opens, but trading immediately is dangerous. Open Interest (OI) data is too small to be reliable. This is not the time to act.

9:15 – 11:00 AM Build Inventory

Pure Observation Period

This window is for building the picture. Watch how OI builds, how price behaves, and where institutional and retail positions are forming.

11:00 AM Act

The Sweet Spot — Calculate PCR

By now, enough institutional and retail positions have been established to reveal the market's true direction. Calculate the Put-Call Ratio (PCR) to decide whether to buy Calls or Puts.

Final Trigger: Wait for VWAP

Once direction is chosen at 11:00 AM, don't enter immediately. Wait for the option price to touch its VWAP (Volume Weighted Average Price) — this ensures you're buying at fair institutional value, not chasing a spike.

Section 03

Handling Gap-Ups & Gap-Downs

When the market opens with a massive gap, early morning data is skewed by traders panic-closing yesterday's positions. The rules shift accordingly.

Normal Day

Standard Execution

  • 9:15Market opens. Begin observation; OI data starts building.
  • 9:15–11:00Observation period. No trades. Build the inventory picture.
  • 11:00Calculate PCR. Decide direction (Calls or Puts).
  • Post-11:00Wait for option price to touch VWAP, then enter.
Gap Up / Gap Down Day

Adjusted Execution

  • OpenMassive gap skews data — early moves driven by panic-closing, not new conviction.
  • 10:00Shift the baseline. Note data from 10:00 AM instead of 9:15 AM.
  • 10:00–13:00Extended observation. Do not rush — let the dust settle.
  • 1:00–2:00 PMEnter only after the new trend is clearly established.
